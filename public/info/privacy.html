Privacy Policy

Last updated: November 20, 2025

Introduction

AnimaID provides an animation center management platform available in two deployment options: self-hosted (managed by you) and hosted (managed by AnimaID on our servers). This Privacy Policy outlines our commitment to privacy principles, provides best practices for data handling, and clarifies our role as the service provider.

Deployment Options:

  • Self-Hosted: You manage your own server infrastructure; data resides entirely within your installation
  • Hosted Service: We provide and maintain the server infrastructure; data is temporarily stored on our secure servers for service operation

Important Disclaimer: Regardless of deployment option, you (the user/administrator) are solely responsible for all personal information collected, stored, and processed within your AnimaID instance. AnimaID provides reliable software and (for hosted services) infrastructure, but does not access, use, or assume responsibility for your data content, privacy compliance, or security measures.

We are committed to building secure, reliable software that enables users to maintain privacy protections. However, all aspects of data protection, security, and regulatory compliance ultimately rest with you, the administrator.

User Responsibility and Disclaimer

By using AnimaID, you acknowledge and accept full responsibility for:

  • All personal information collected, stored, and processed within your installation
  • Implementing appropriate privacy protections and security measures
  • Ensuring compliance with all applicable local, national, and international privacy laws
  • Protecting against data breaches, unauthorized access, or security incidents
  • Managing data retention and deletion policies

AnimaID and its providers accept no liability or responsibility for any data security incidents, privacy violations, legal non-compliance, or damages resulting from the use of this software or service in any deployment (self-hosted or hosted). Users must independently assess and mitigate their own risks. You are strongly advised to consult with qualified legal and security professionals to ensure your implementation meets all requirements.

Potential Information Types

AnimaID does not collect or store any personal information as a service provider. However, as the administrator of your AnimaID instance (self-hosted or hosted), you may collect and store the following types of information depending on your configuration and usage:

Personal Information

  • Staff member names, contact information, and role assignments
  • Children and family member information for registration and safety purposes
  • Account credentials and authentication data
  • Contact information for internal support requests

Usage Data

  • Activity attendance records
  • System access logs and timestamps
  • Communication records within the platform
  • Document and media file management logs

How Users May Use Information

As a service provider, AnimaID does not use or access your data. How information is used within your instance depends entirely on your organization's purposes and how you configure the system. You may use collected information for legitimate purposes such as:

  • Managing animation center operations and user accounts
  • Tracking attendance and activity participation
  • Facilitating communication between staff, families, and administration
  • Ensuring child safety and regulatory compliance
  • Providing support and maintaining operational records
  • Meeting educational and administrative requirements

Information Sharing by Users

AnimaID does not store, control, or share any personal information as a service provider. All decisions regarding information sharing are made by you, the user administrator. You may choose to share information with third parties only if necessary and with appropriate safeguards, such as:

  • With explicit individual consent
  • To comply with legal obligations or regulatory requirements
  • To protect individual rights, property, or safety
  • In connection with emergency situations or legal proceedings

Security Features in AnimaID

We develop AnimaID with built-in security features to help protect data in deployments. These may include encryption for sensitive data, secure authentication systems, access controls, and audit logging capabilities. However, you, the user, are solely responsible for: configuring these security features appropriately, keeping the software and underlying systems up-to-date, implementing organizational security measures, and maintaining overall protection against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

Data Retention (User Responsibility)

You, as the administrator of your AnimaID instance, determine how long information is retained in your system. This decision should be based on your operational needs, legal requirements, and privacy best practices. We recommend regularly reviewing and establishing clear retention schedules. Children's information should be retained only as necessary for your educational and safety purposes, in compliance with local child protection regulations.

Managing Individual Rights

AnimaID provides administrative tools to help you manage individual data rights within your platform. Depending on your location and applicable privacy laws, you should implement support for rights such as:

  • The right to access personal information held about them
  • The right to correct inaccurate or incomplete information
  • The right to delete personal information (subject to legal and operational requirements)
  • The right to restrict or object to further processing
  • The right to data portability (exporting their information)

As the software provider, we provide the tools; you are responsible for proper implementation and compliance.

Children's Privacy (User Compliance)

AnimaID is designed with features to facilitate responsible handling of children's information. We are committed to providing software that supports privacy protections, but as the user and administrator, you are fully responsible for ensuring compliance with all applicable children's privacy laws and regulations worldwide.

This includes obtaining appropriate parental consent before collecting or processing children's information, limiting collection to legitimate educational and safety purposes only, and adhering to laws such as COPPA (United States), GDPR (European Union), or other regional children's privacy protections. Consult legal professionals familiar with your jurisdiction to ensure proper implementation.
